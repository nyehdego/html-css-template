The Vending Machine

Vending machines are a common sight around college campuses, and many students make the time to go grab their favorite snack. Although interacting seems simple, what do we find when we pay closer attention to interactions with them? Here is a sketch of a vending machine from the Sciences Library at Brown University.

Vending Machine Sketch and Annotation

What can we say about this interface?

This interface is built to help users easily identify and choose a variety of items through snack labels. It also tries to provide clear instructions for vending and transaction methods with a screen displaying these instructions, and labels for the various components.

Creating Personas

Let's create two personas based on our observations and interviews by creating an empathy map for both.

First Persona

Empathy Map: Mark, The Time Conscience Student

Feels

Slightly anxious when tasks take longer than expected. Satisfied when processes are seamless.

Says

"I need to get this done quickly so I can move on."

"WHy is this taking so long?."

Thinks

Prioritizes efficiency and simplicity in everyday tasks. Considers whether things are worth the time and cost.

Does

Acts decisively, avoids unecessary steps, and quickly moves on to the next task.

Interface Problems This Persona Faces

  • Lack of clear pricing display without having to input product code.
  • Inconsistent payment methods (does not accept school card like other machines).
  • Poor error messaging when machine is out of service.

Why This Persona Represents Users

  • Mark captures the majority of the users we observed who priortize speed and convenience. Our observations of Andrew and Ryan align with this persona, where users expect simple interactions and feel frustration when the machine fails to deliver quickly.

Second Persona

Empathy Map: Ashley, The (Very) Social Friend

Feels

Excited when collaborating with others, but annoyed when decisions take too long.

Says

"Ohh, that's a good one!"

"Let's try something new."

Thinks

Prefers shared experiences and options that offer variety. Enjoys exploring different things.

Does

Enganges in conversations, considers multiple options, and enjoys decision-making as part of a group activity.

Interface Problems This Persona Faces

  • Confusing instructions for first-time users.
  • Lack of clear feedback on failed transactions

Why This Persona Represents Users

  • Ashley represents users who engage with the interface in a more social, exploratory way. The experiences of Eliana and Alina reflect how vending machines can cause confusion and frustration for users unfamiliar with the payment process or expecting more interactive elements.

Finally, let's storyboard our first persona's (Mark) interaction with the vending machine

Mark sees the vending machine in the hallway between classes and walks towards it. He's in a rush, looking at his phone.

Mark quickly scans the product options behind the glass, searching for a granola bar.

Mark realizes there are no visible prices on products themselves, requiring him to see the price on the screen.

Mark types the code for the granola bar, glancing back at his phone.

Mark swipes his student card without hesitation, expecting an instant transaction.

An error message appears: "Card Not Accepted".

Mark tries swiping the card again, but the same error message appears. He sighs in frustration.

After a few seconds of waiting, he gives up and walks away, still hungry and disappointed. Mark thinks about how the machine should clearly display prices and accepted payment methods.
